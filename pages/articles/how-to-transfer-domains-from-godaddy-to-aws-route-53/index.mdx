            <Header>How to Transfer `.com` Domains from GoDaddy to AWS Route 53</Header>

            If you're planning to move your domain registration from GoDaddy to AWS Route 53, you may be wondering how to get started. Fortunately, transferring domains is a straightforward process that can be completed in just a few steps.

            In this blog post, we'll show you how to transfer .com, .co.uk, and .uk domains from GoDaddy to AWS Route 53, including examples and links.

            ## Prerequisites

            Before you begin, you'll need to have the following:

            - An AWS account <a href={"https://console.aws.amazon.com/console/home?nc2=h_ct&src=header-signin"} target={"_blank"} rel={"noreferrer"}>here</a>
            - A domain name that you want to transfer from GoDaddy to Route 53 <a href={"https://www.godaddy.com/products"} target={"_blank"} rel={"noreferrer"}>here</a>
            - Access to the email address associated with the domain's administrative contact in the WHOIS database

            ## Step 1: Unlock Your Domain
            Before you can transfer your domain, you'll need to unlock it in your GoDaddy account. Here's how:

            1. Log in to your GoDaddy account.
            2. Go to the "Domains" section of your account. <a href={"https://dcc.godaddy.com/products"} target={"_blank"} rel={"noreferrer"}>here</a>
            3. Find the domain you want to transfer and click on its name.
            4. Click on Manage Domain in Quick Links section (on the right side).

            <br/>
            <Image src={manageDomains} alt="Manage Domains" />

            ## Step 2: Get Your Authorization Code

            To transfer your domain to Route 53, you'll need an authorization code (also known as an EPP code or transfer code) from GoDaddy. Here's how to get it:

            1. Scroll down to the "Additional Settings" section and click "Transfer Domain Away From GoDaddy".
            2. Follow the prompts to get your authorization code.

            <br/>
            <Image src={transferDomain} alt="Transfer Domain Away" />

            ## Step 3: Start the Transfer in Route 53

            Now that you have your authorization code, you can start the transfer process in AWS Route 53. Here's how:

            1. Log in to your AWS account and go to the Route 53 console.
            2. Click on "Registered domains" in the left-hand menu.
            3. Click "Transfer domain" and enter the domain name you want to transfer.
            4. Follow the prompts to complete the transfer process.

            <br/>
            <Image src={registeredDomains} alt="Registered Domains" />

            ## Step 4: Verify Your Email Address

            After you initiate the transfer in Route 53, you'll receive an email from Amazon to the administrative contact email address associated with your domain. Follow the instructions in the email to verify your email address.

            ## Step 5: Wait for the Transfer to Complete

            Once you've verified your email address, the transfer process will begin. It can take up to seven days for the transfer to complete, but it's usually much faster than that. During this time, your domain will still be accessible on GoDaddy, but you won't be able to make any changes to it.

            ## Step 6: Update Your DNS Records

            Once the transfer is complete, you'll need to update your DNS records to point to your AWS resources. Here's how:

            1. Log in to your AWS account and go to the Route 53 console.
            2. Click on "Hosted zones" in the left-hand menu.
            3. Click on the hosted zone that corresponds to your domain.
            4. Update your DNS records to point to your AWS resources.

            And that's it! You've successfully transferred your domain from GoDaddy

            Note: It may take up to 7 Days for the transfer to complete.
